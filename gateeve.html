Gateway of India

📖 History

The Gateway of India was built in 1924 to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to Bombay in 1911. It became a ceremonial entrance to India for Viceroys and Governors.

📍 Location & How to Reach

  • Location: Colaba, South Mumbai
  • Nearest Stations: CST, Churchgate
  • Transport: Taxi, Bus, Local Train

🕒 Timings & Fees

  • Open 24 Hours
  • Entry Fee: Free
  • Ferry Rides: ₹150–₹300 (for Elephanta Caves)

📸 What to See

  • Majestic basalt archway
  • Stunning sea view
  • Taj Mahal Palace Hotel nearby

🚢 Activities

  • Photography
  • Street food and shopping
  • Ferry ride to Elephanta Island

📌 Nearby Attractions

  • Colaba Causeway Market
  • Marine Drive
  • CST Station

💡 Visitor Tips

  • Visit during sunset for beautiful views
  • Keep your belongings secure in crowds
  • Negotiate prices with street photographers

🧐 Did You Know?

The Gateway of India was the exit point of the last British troops in 1948, marking the end of British rule in India.
